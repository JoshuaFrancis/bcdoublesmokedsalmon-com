Three deliberate acts, and a great deal of patience.

Most smoked salmon is hurried. Ours is not. The fish is cured by hand, then drawn through two woods, one cold, one warm, until the flavour is layered the way a good record is.

  1. Cured

    A dry rub of Pacific sea salt, demerara, and bay. Seventy-two hours, undisturbed. The flesh tightens; the colour deepens to garnet.

  2. Cold-smoked, alder

    Alder gives a quiet, almost floral smoke. Twenty hours below 28°C, long enough for the wood to enter the fish without ever cooking it.

  3. Hot-smoked, cherry

    Cherry is sweeter, redder, more present. A short, hot finish that lacquers the surface and sets the second flavour against the first.

From a coast that has been doing this for a very long time.

The salmon is hand-line caught in the cold, mineral water between Haida Gwaii and the mainland. The same channels first-nations smokehouses have worked for generations. We buy small. We buy from skippers we know. The fish is on ice within minutes and in our cure room within a day.
